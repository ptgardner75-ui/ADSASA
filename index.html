Who We Are

ADSASA is an independent standards and accreditation body for Audio Description (AD), originating in South Africa and designed for global adoption and interoperability.

We define, evaluate, and certify Audio Description practices across broadcast, streaming, film, cinema, advertising, and live media environments.

Our Mandate

“Nothing About Us Without Us”

Accessibility is a human right, not a service. No Audio Description shall be considered compliant unless it has been shaped, reviewed, and verified with the direct participation of blind and visually impaired users.

What We Do

  • Develop Audio Description standards and best practice frameworks
  • Certify compliant Audio Description production
  • Audit accessibility quality in media content
  • Support broadcasters, streaming platforms, and production houses
  • Promote inclusive and equitable media access globally

Standards Approach

ADSASA standards combine technical precision, editorial clarity, and lived user experience. All frameworks are designed to ensure consistency, intelligibility, and real-world usability.

  • Clear editorial hierarchy of visual information
  • Objective, present-tense descriptive language
  • Technical audio delivery requirements
  • Community-based verification and testing

Global Vision

Although developed in South Africa, ADSASA is designed as a global accessibility framework. It aligns with international best practices and is intended for adoption across all major media markets.

Our goal is simple: to make Audio Description consistent, verifiable, and meaningful worldwide.
