ADSASA

Audio Description Standards Accreditation and Services Authority

A global standards body for Audio Description accessibility

Who We Are

ADSASA is an independent standards and accreditation body for Audio Description (AD), originating in South Africa and designed for global adoption and interoperability.

We exist to define, elevate, and certify Audio Description practices across broadcast, streaming, film, cinema, advertising, and live media.

Our Mandate

"Nothing About Us Without Us"

Accessibility is a human right, not a service. No Audio Description shall be considered compliant unless it has been shaped, reviewed, and verified with the direct participation of blind and visually impaired users.

What We Do

  • Define global Audio Description standards
  • Certify compliant AD production
  • Audit accessibility quality in media
  • Support broadcasters and streaming platforms
  • Promote inclusive media practices worldwide

Standards Framework

ADSASA operates a structured compliance system covering:

  • Editorial and scripting standards
  • Technical audio delivery requirements
  • Community verification processes
  • Live and automated AD systems

Global Vision

Although developed in South Africa, ADSASA is designed as a global framework, aligned with international accessibility best practices and adaptable across all major media markets.
